    Performance over time¶

    The trend is clearly upward rather than flat midfield performance. My median final score improved from 39% in 2024 to 57% in 2025 and 63% in 2026 so far. In practical terms, my typical result moved from the lower half of the field to roughly the top third.

    Qualifying medians: 44% in 2024, 59% in 2025 and 62% in 2026. Final medians: 39% in 2024, 57% in 2025 and 63% in 2026.

    Event-by-event performance¶

    This chart shows every completed event with an exact published final ranking. Hover or tap a point to see the event, date and original placement. The dashed line is a trailing five-race moving average, which reduces event-to-event noise. Official DCS weekend results are counted once; their individual multi-final rounds, unfinished events and result ranges are omitted.

    Event-by-event final performance and its trailing five-race moving average trend upward overall between 2024 and 2026.

    In 2026, 7 of 9 event results (78%) ended in the top half, compared with 1 of 3 (33%) in 2024. The median final score rises from 39% → 57% → 63% across the three seasons.

    Individual results still vary with race format and field strength, so this is not a straight line or a formal skill rating. The season-level direction is positive.

    The score is normalized for field size: (field size - position) / (field size - 1) × 100. A winner scores 100, exact midfield is around 50 and last place scores 0. Each standalone competition counts once. For a DCS multi-final weekend, only the official combined event result counts toward the final median; its round results remain visible in the table but are not counted again. Qualifying medians use the published round-level tables because the combined reports do not provide one overall qualifying rank. Fun races, unfinished events, season standings and finals without an exact position are excluded. n is the number of results included.

    How to read the tables

    Each position is linked to its source. A dash means that the event format or published result did not include a separate leaderboard for that stage. When a timing platform and a consolidated event result differ, the consolidated result is used. Bold DCS rows are the official result for the complete event. The indented rows below them are the individual multi-final rounds that feed into that result. Where the format uses a dropped round, the official PDF already applies that rule and any tie-breakers. Raw timing-system views can count DNS or unclassified pilots differently.
