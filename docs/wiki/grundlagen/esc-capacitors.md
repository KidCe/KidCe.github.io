# ESC capacitors

An ESC capacitor helps absorb voltage spikes and electrical noise generated by the battery leads, ESC and motors. This reduces stress on the electronics and can improve the reliability of the power system and video feed.

## What to use

- Choose a good-quality **low-ESR electrolytic capacitor**.
- Its voltage rating must be higher than the fully charged battery voltage, with sensible safety margin.
- Use a capacitance appropriate for the build and ESC manufacturer's recommendation.
- Never exceed the capacitor's temperature and voltage ratings.

For a typical 6S 5-inch build, a quality 35V low-ESR capacitor is a common starting point. Larger or particularly noisy setups may benefit from more capacitance, provided the installation remains safe and practical.

## Installation

Connect the capacitor across the ESC battery input:

- Positive capacitor leg to battery positive
- Negative capacitor leg, marked by the stripe on an electrolytic capacitor, to battery negative
- Keep the leads as short as practical
- Insulate exposed conductors and secure the capacitor against vibration and crashes

!!! danger "Polarity matters"
    Electrolytic capacitors are polarized. Connecting one backwards or exceeding its voltage rating can destroy it and may cause it to vent or burst.

## Capacitors are not a cure-all

A capacitor can reduce spikes and noise, but it cannot compensate for damaged components, poor solder joints, undersized wiring or an overloaded ESC. If failures or resets continue, inspect the entire power system rather than relying on a larger capacitor alone.
